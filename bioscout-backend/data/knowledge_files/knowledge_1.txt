# Birds of Margalla Hills

The Margalla Hills National Park is a birding paradise, housing approximately 250-300 bird species. Among these, 73 species are resident while the rest are migratory. 

Common resident birds include the Grey Francolin, Himalayan Griffon, White-cheeked Bulbul, Himalayan Bulbul, Rufous Treepie, Oriental Turtle Dove, Spotted Dove, and Red-vented Bulbul.

During winter months (October to March), migratory birds from Central Asia and Siberia arrive, including various species of ducks, geese, cranes, waders, and passerines. 

Some of the rare birds spotted in the Margalla Hills include the Peregrine Falcon, Laggar Falcon, Himalayan Griffon Vulture, Egyptian Vulture, White-backed Vulture, and Tawny Eagle, all of which are of conservation concern.
